Mastering the Balancing Act

Posted on February 6, 2018February 6, 2018 by L

 

Hello, everyone! I’ve been so busy lately, it’s a bit insane – I’m not doing as many activities as I did in high school, but everything I do now has higher stakes. Suddenly, I’m juggling two jobs, a research assistant position, four classes at a top university, volunteer work, and starting my application for graduate school. All of these responsibilities are important to me and my future career. On top of this, I’m also trying to have a social life, eat healthy, exercise regularly, and sleep eight hours, daily habits that are important for my mental and physical health.

Sometimes, I feel as though I’m balancing on a tight-rope, and as long as I keep walking and don’t look down, I’ll be fine. If I stop for just a second, though, to catch my breath, I’ll topple and lose everything I’m working so hard to accomplish.

My advice for you, if you are in a similarly precarious state-of-mind, is to BREATHE. 

Think about the choices you are making, right here and right now. Think about why you are making those choices. Sometimes, this introspection helps me realize that I’m actually procrastinating, which definitely doesn’t help the situation. Other times, remembering that I’m working hard so that I can build my dream life is inspiring, and helps motivate me.

Once you verify your actions and purposes, just start working. Slowly but surely, start ticking off the items on your to-do list, and don’t stop until you truly feel like you’ve finished at least half of what you set out to do today.

Life is short, and you definitely shouldn’t spend it unhappy and with high blood pressure. On the other hand, sometimes you just have to plow through the guts to achieve the glory. Don’t worry, though, I believe you can accomplish anything you set your mind to, and a good work ethic makes all the difference between dreams and reality.
