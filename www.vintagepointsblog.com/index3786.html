A Few of My Favourite Christmas Things

Posted on December 4, 2018 by L

Ahh, it’s December! I don’t care how old and cynical I become, Christmas will always remain the most wonderful time of year, and I will always be excited for the month of December. Is it just me, or did 2018 just fly by? It feels like only yesterday we were celebrating Halloween, and now here we are at the second-to-last week of the fall semester and the beginning of winter. Well, at any rate, below are a few of my favourite things to do during the holiday season – let me know in the comments below what activities you enjoy, and happy Monday, everyone!

1. Christmas carols

I was raised on old Hollywood musicals and Disney movies, so, really, my obsession with Christmas carols shouldn’t come as a surprise to anyone. I love blasting my Spotify playlist whenever I’m working in the lab, walking to class, or even when I’m wandering around town looking at the Christmas lights. My personal favourite melody is “The Christmas Waltz”, but “All I Want for Christmas is You” and “I Want a Hippopotamus for Christmas” are close contenders.

2. Gingerbread house making

Full disclosure, I made my first gingerbread house last year with my best friend, at the ripe old age of eighteen. So, this wasn’t a big part of my childhood – nevertheless, I made another little cottage this week, and totally loved being creativity on a three-dimensional level. Plus, the fact that everything was edible really made my stomach happy… ðŸ™‚

3. Christmas movies

I made a whole blog post about this last year – and, for the record, I will forever defend Die Hard as a Christmas movie. My family has also been watching Christmas with the Kranks for at least the last decade, and I can’t wait to go home for winter break so that I can watch it with them again this year. I know that the kids these days are obsessed with The Christmas Prince, but, to be honest, I haven’t watched it and I don’t know if I will – but, who knows, anything is possible during Christmas time!

4. Peppermint hot chocolate

Don’t even talk to me about Starbucks’ peppermint mocha nonsense – my mom makes the BEST hot cocoa drink. She uses dark chocolate, peppermint extract, and then tops it with whipped cream, chocolate sauce, and crushed candy canes. Ahh, my mouth is watering at just the thought – she also makes a really good caramel hot chocolate, so maybe she’s just a good beverage inventor?

5. Spending time with family

Speaking of my mom, the best part of the holidays is definitely, by far, spending time with my parents. I’m an only child, and extended family is, well, extended across all the continents and American states, so it’s always been just us three during the Christmas season. The older I get, the more I appreciate spending time with those I love, and the more I treasure our dorky Christmas traditions, like leaving out carrots for Santa’s reindeer, reusing all the wrapping paper, and hand-making all our holiday cards.
