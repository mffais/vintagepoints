10 Things I Want to Do Over Winter Break

Posted on December 11, 2018December 11, 2018 by L

Hey y’all, happy Monday! Today has been a busy but productive day, with most of my time spent at work, and I just finished a four-hour study session with a friend. My last final is this Wednesday, so fingers crossed I do well 🙂

On that note, I definitely want to stay intellectually stimulated and academically motivated over my five week winter break. So, below are some goals that I’ve set for myself to accomplish over the holiday. Of course, I’m also going to be spending time with loved ones, trying out new restaurants that opened up while I was gone, etc., but all in all I’m really excited to enjoy the last month of the year.

1. learn personal finance

Ahh, I tried to do this in high school, but unfortunately AP macroeconomics doesn’t really teach you about how to choose a bank, compound interest, 401k plans, etc. Since I’ll be graduating this May, and going off into the big, wide world of adulthood, I figure that I need to finally learn how to take care of my finances. Luckily, there are a bunch of free online resources available, as well as some books at my local library, that I can take advantage of over the holiday.

2. revisit old languages

I LOVE learning languages, but this semester has been brutal, and I haven’t had a chance to do anything except focus on schoolwork. So, this winter break I’m going to revisit and relearn German, French, Chinese, Italian, and Spanish – hopefully I’ll remember some of what I used to know, and I can get back to the semi-proficient level I was once at.

3. read more books

My eyesight has gotten steadily worse over the last few months, and I’m pretty sure it’s because I’ve been in front of a computer day in and day out. So, although I love television, I’m going to make sure to pick up some good, old-fashioned books in the next few weeks. Right now, I’m looking for some quality content across all genres, so hopefully I’ll churn out some book reviews for you guys!

4. draw some large-scale paintings

I haven’t had the time lately to draw, but during the break I’ll have buckets of hours to spend on some huge canvases. I actually really want to experiment with oil painting this month, since I haven’t done that in a while, and I’m hoping to do some photo-realistic work. Believe it or not, I was once considered quite decent at art, and I don’t want to lose that passion to the monotony of everyday life.

5. volunteer

I’m so excited to return to my old stomping grounds and get back to volunteering in my community! I have been helping out at my local museum here at university, but there’s something about volunteering in a community that has known you for years that makes me really happy. I can’t wait to spread some holiday cheer and reconnect with people I care about.

6. make a bullet journal

I’ve thought about making a bullet journal for a couple of years now, but I’ve always put it off because I had ‘more important things to worry about’. Well, now that I’ve graduated high school, gotten into university, and sent off my grad school applications, I can really focus on the things that make me happy, such as being creative and planning out my day.

7. try new forms of exercise

Hmm, this one might be tricky, but I’m still going to try new forms of exercise. Right now, I’m thinking of doing yoga every morning, but I might also try pilates – my back has been hurting lately from being hunched over at a desk all the time, and I want to make sure I can still walk when I’m 80.

8. cook

I miss cooking so much!!! OMG, I didn’t realize how much I enjoyed cooking until I had to live in a dormitory for a full year. I honestly cannot wait to make poached eggs, instant noodles, and maybe even peppermint bark.

9. plan for the summer

I don’t know what I’m going to do this summer, and although that unknowing is a bit intimidating, it’s mostly exciting. I’m hoping to visit Europe with a friend, as well as visit my grandparents, but in order to do that I have to plan, budget, and figure out schedules – over the holidays, I’ll have plenty of time to fine-tune the details.

10. chill

Truly, honestly, chill. I’ve been constantly overstimulated here at university, and although I’ve definitely matured and had wonderful experiences, I’ve also had moments where I just want to sleep for 50 hours, wake up for food, and sleep for 50 more. Hopefully, during the break, I can sleep, do spa days, watch cheesy action films with my family, and relax to my heart’s content.
